What is parapsychology?

Parapsychology is the study of mental phenomena that fall outside the scope of orthodox psychology and seem, at first glance, to be impossible. This includes things like telepathy, precognition, clairvoyance, psychokinesis, and other paranormal claims.





Skeptical?

Good! You should be. Paranormal claims, by definition, seem to be incompatible with our current understanding of the world. The scientific community generally considers parapsychology to be pseudo-science, and this is partly because many parapsychologists have been guilty of sloppy experiment design, faulty analysis, and having a gullible mindset in general. The stereotypical parapsychologist is someone who attributes anything they can't understand to ghosts and aliens, someone who sees a magic trick and thinks it's the real thing. And this is actually true of some of the people in the field.

And it certainly doesn't help that some (probably most) of the high-profile cases of people claiming to have paranormal abilities turn out to be frauds.

My point is that you'd have to be pretty dumb to start believing in stuff like telepathy and psychokinesis just because some guy on the internet says it's true. At the moment there still isn't enough research to prove conclusively, beyond a doubt, that any of this stuff is real. If you're science-minded, then I'm sure you'll demand not only successful experiments under air-tight conditions, but also numerous replications of these experiments by independent labs. Settling for anything less would be overly credulous and unscientific.

So why do I believe in any of this stuff? Because I've actually witnessed it. I was a skeptic right up until I saw telepathic communication happening right in front of me. Should you take my word for this? Absolutely not! I'm just a guy on the internet, and have no reason to trust me. My goal with this webpage isn't to convince people that paranormal phenomena are real, but rather to encourage people to find out for themselves. For this stuff to actually be taken seriously by the mainstream scientific community, the stuff I've seen that convinced me will have to be widely replicated by independent researchers in several different labs. As far as I'm concerned, that's the next step.
